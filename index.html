📚 Game Documentation

Everything you need to know about Ncho

How to Play

Ncho is a two-player strategy game played on a board with 12 pots arranged in two rows. Each pot starts with 4 stones.

Objective

The goal is to capture more stones than your opponent by strategically distributing and collecting stones across the board.

Game Rules

Rule Description
Starting the Game Each of the 12 pots contains 4 stones at the start. Player goes first.
Making a Move Select a pot from your side (6 pots). All stones from that pot are distributed one by one into subsequent pots clockwise.
Capturing Stones If the last stone lands in a pot making it contain exactly 4 stones, you capture all 4 stones.
Continuing Play If the last stone lands in a non-empty pot (not making 4), pick up all stones from that pot and continue distributing.
Ending a Turn Your turn ends when the last stone lands in an empty pot.
End of Round A round ends when 4 or fewer stones remain on the board. The last player to capture takes the remaining stones.
Winning The player who captures the most stones wins the round. Win best of 5 rounds to win the game!

Controls

Touch/Click

Tap on any pot with stones on your side to select it and distribute the stones.

Pause

Tap the pause button or press the back button to pause the game.

Settings

Access game settings to adjust difficulty, sound, and other options.

Game Features Explained

AI Difficulty Levels

  • Easy: AI makes random moves. Perfect for learning the game.
  • Medium: AI tries to capture stones when possible. Good for practice.
  • Hard: AI uses strategic planning and looks ahead. Real challenge!

Save & Continue

Your game is automatically saved when you pause or return to the main menu. Resume anytime from where you left off!

Visual Feedback

  • Yellow Highlight: Pot you're hovering over (can be selected)
  • Green Highlight: Pot you've selected
  • Red Glow: Pot selected by the Keeper (AI opponent)
  • Orange Flash: Stone dropped into a pot

Tips & Strategies

💡 Plan Ahead

Think about where your last stone will land before making a move.

🎯 Aim for Captures

Try to land your last stone in pots with 3 stones to capture 4.

🛡️ Defensive Play

Avoid leaving pots with 3 stones on your side that opponent can capture.

🔄 Chain Moves

Landing in non-empty pots lets you continue - use this strategically!
